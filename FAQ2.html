What is HDR photography?

HDR stands for High Dynamic Range, the camera is set up on a tripod to take three shots in a row.
One of the images is normal exposure (normal lighting), one is darker, and one is really bright.
The pictures are then processed with special software that blends the 3 exposures together.
Blending them together makes a HDR photo and reveals the best of each exposure, the darks, lights, and normal lighting.
This makes the image more similar to what you would see with the human eye when standing in the room.

In other words, in a normal photo there are areas where the photo is too light or too dark.
In the darker exposed images, it brings down the brightness of it all.
In the light exposure images, you can see the dark areas better.
So blending all these together makes for a perfect image where no areas are too bright and no areas are too dark!
